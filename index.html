Going further

Open what you need, ignore the rest.

Best resources (free → paid)+
  • Free: TV5Monde « Apprendre », RFI slow-news podcast, InnerFrench, Coffee Break French, Anki (flashcards).
  • Speaking: an AI app (Langua/Speak) daily; italki for a weekly human tutor ($8–30/hr).
  • Structure: Babbel or Busuu (apps), Lingoda (live classes), Alliance Française or Cours Municipaux (in person).
Cost & funding (if you're in France)+
  • CPF — if you're on a French payroll, ~€500/year can fund a course and the DELF exam.
  • Employer budget — many companies reimburse language classes. Just ask.
  • OFII / Cours Municipaux — free or very cheap state & city courses (depends on your permit).
Certification: DELF B2 & citizenship+

Get the DELF B2 diploma — it's permanent (never expires), ~€150–280 once. Better than the TCF/TEF tests, which lapse after 2 years.

For French citizenship you also need ~5 years' residence, a civic test (40 questions, pass 32/40), and an interview in French. Always check service-public.fr for current rules.

A real example: two beginners in Paris+

Ekta & Kartik moved to Paris as near-beginners — Kartik stuck at A1 for years because he lived in English and leaned on translation apps. The fix was free: "try first, then translate," and make home a French zone. They study together, practise daily with AI, and aim for DELF B2 — Kartik for citizenship. The lesson: find your real blocker (time? a habit?) and aim your hours at it.
